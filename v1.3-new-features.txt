Traveller World & System Generator — What's New in v1.3.0
==========================================================

ADVANCED TEMPERATURE (NEW IN GEN-UI)
--------------------------------------
- A new "Advanced temperature" checkbox under "Full detail" enables physics-based
  temperature calculations for the mainworld.

- Advanced Mean Temperature uses the formula T = 279 × ⁴√(L × (1-A) × (1+G) / AU²),
  where L is the combined luminosity of stars interior to the world's orbit, A is the
  rolled surface albedo, and G is the rolled greenhouse factor.

  Albedo is determined by world type (rocky, icy, or icy-far, based on density and
  orbital distance), then modified by atmosphere type and hydrographics.

  Greenhouse factor is based on atmospheric pressure, scaled by whether the atmosphere
  is standard, exotic, or extreme.

- High Temperature and Low Temperature are also computed from the same albedo and
  greenhouse values, using a 9-step seasonal variance procedure (WBH pp.48-50):
    • Axial Tilt Factor — how much the axial tilt varies temperature across seasons
    • Rotation Factor — how day length affects temperature distribution
    • Geographic Factor — how land/ocean ratio affects temperature swing
    • Luminosity Modifier — variance modulated by atmospheric buffering
  High temperature uses the near-periastron distance; low temperature uses the
  far-apastron distance, with eccentricity from the world's orbit.

- Albedo, greenhouse factor, advanced mean, high, and low temperatures all appear
  in the world card below the basic mean temperature row.

NATIVE LIFE — BIOCOMPLEXITY AND SOPHONTS
------------------------------------------
- Every world or moon with a positive biomass rating now also receives a
  Biocomplexity Rating (minimum 1) that describes the most advanced organisms
  possible. This is based on biomass, atmosphere type, system age, and whether
  any low-oxygen taint is present. The orbit table shows both ratings in a new
  "Biosphere" column (e.g. "B4, C3" for Biomass 4, Biocomplexity 3).

- Worlds with biocomplexity 8 or higher now roll to determine whether a native
  sophont species exists. A "Native sophont" row appears in the world card:
    • "Extant" — a currently living sophont species is present.
    • "Extinct (evidence present)" — a past sophont species left traces behind.
  The extinct roll also gets a bonus for old systems (age > 5 Gyr), reflecting
  the longer window for a civilisation to have risen and fallen.

NATIVE LIFE — BIOMASS RATING
------------------------------
- Biomass ratings are now generated for all terrestrial worlds and moons in a
  system when "Full detail" is enabled. A "Biomass N" note appears in the
  Notes column of the orbit table for any world or moon with a positive rating.

- The mainworld's biomass rating is always shown in the World Body detail card
  when "Full detail" is enabled (regardless of whether life is present).

- New optional rule: enable "Oxygen requires biomass" to ensure that any world
  with an oxygenated atmosphere (codes 2–9, D, E) has at least biomass 1, even
  if the dice roll would produce no life.

MOON ORBITS — ECCENTRICITY AND INCLINATION
-------------------------------------------
- Significant moons with orbital positions now have eccentricity and inclination
  rolled using the same tables as world orbits (WBH pp.27–28). Both values appear
  in the Ecc/Incl column of the orbit table moon sub-rows, formatted as
  "0.123/45.6°".

- A moon inclination greater than 90° indicates a retrograde orbit (the moon
  orbits opposite to the planet's rotation), which is now implicit from the
  angle rather than a separate yes/no flag.

- Eccentricity and inclination are emitted in JSON output (as orbit_eccentricity
  and orbit_inclination) when non-zero.

HYDROGRAPHIC DETAIL
--------------------
- The Hydrographic detail card now shows a Fluid type row alongside Surface
  liquid percentage. The fluid type is determined by the world's temperature
  zone (WBH pp.91–92):
    • Boiling worlds: Sulfuric Acid
    • Hot and Temperate worlds: Water
    • Cold worlds: Ammonia
    • Frozen worlds: Liquid Hydrocarbons
  Desert worlds (Hydrographics 0) and gas/hydrogen atmosphere worlds show no
  fluid type. Fluid type is emitted in JSON output as "fluid_type" within the
  hydrographics.detail object when present.

WORLD PHYSICAL DETAIL
----------------------
- Surface tidal amplitude is now calculated and displayed in the World Body
  detail card (e.g. "Tidal amplitude: 30.84 m"). This is the combined tidal
  effect from the primary star and all significant moons acting on the world's
  surface.

- Tidal Stress Factor is now shown in the World Body detail card. This is the
  seismic stress contribution from surface tidal forces (tidal amplitude in
  metres ÷ 10, rounded down). It contributes to the Total Seismic Stress.

- Seismic stress is displayed as three distinct components in the World Body
  detail card:
    • Tidal Seismic Stress — contribution from the world's orbital eccentricity
    • Tidal Stress Factor — contribution from surface tidal forces
    • Residual Seismic Stress — contribution from the world's age, size, and density
  Total Seismic Stress is the sum of all three. Where internal heating raises the
  effective temperature, the Seismic Temperature is also shown.

- The stellar day (solar day) is now calculated and displayed below the sidereal
  day (rotation period) in the World Body detail card. The stellar day is the
  time between successive sunrises — what inhabitants experience as "a day". It
  differs from the sidereal rotation period because the world moves along its
  orbit while rotating.

  The correction follows WBH p.106:
    • Prograde worlds: stellar day > sidereal day
    • Retrograde worlds: stellar day < sidereal day
    • Tidally locked worlds (1:1 lock): no stellar day row is shown (the star is
      stationary in the sky)
    • 3:2 resonance (Mercury-like): stellar day ≈ 2 × orbital period

  The stellar day is only shown when orbital data is available (i.e., when
  generating a full system with Physical detail enabled).

GEN-UI CONTROLS
----------------
- The "System detail" and "Mainworld detail" checkboxes have been replaced with
  a pair of radio buttons:
    • "Mainworld only" (default) — generates a single world with full atmosphere,
      hydrographic, and physical detail.
    • "Full detail" — generates a complete star system with secondary worlds,
      moons, biomass, biocomplexity, sophont checks, and the system map.

- System generation results now use a two-tab layout:
    • "System" tab — stellar data and the full orbit table.
    • "Mainworld" tab — the mainworld HTML card (default tab).

- The "NHZ Atmospheres", "Oxygen requires biomass", and "Advanced temperature"
  checkboxes are only available under Full detail, and are cleared automatically
  when switching back to Mainworld only.

- The "Orbital Eccentricity" and "Orbital Inclination" checkboxes have been
  removed. Both values are now always calculated when Full detail is enabled,
  so the Ecc/Incl column is always populated.

SYSTEM MAP & ORBIT TABLE
------------------------
- The mainworld row in the orbit table is now always displayed in bold,
  including when the mainworld is a satellite of a gas giant.

BUG FIXES
---------
- Fixed: biomass temperature penalty for boiling and frozen worlds was too low.
  The WBH p.127 table has five temperature rows (two for High temperature, three
  for Mean temperature); only the Mean temperature rows were being applied. Both
  sections now fire independently, so a boiling or frozen world correctly
  receives DM-6 instead of DM-4 or DM-2.

- Fixed: a crash that occurred when generating physical detail for a belt
  mainworld in a system that also had moon tidal effects to apply.
